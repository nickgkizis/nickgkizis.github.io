Coding and the Humanities: A Complex Coexistence

The relationship between coding and the humanities represents a fascinating intersection of two distinct intellectual domains. While both fields aim to solve problems and expand knowledge, the methodologies and challenges associated with each differ profoundly.

Challenges in the Traditional Humanities

Traditional humanities disciplines, such as literature, philosophy, and history, demand extensive studying and deep immersion in vast amounts of material. I recall a particular exam during my literature studies where I had to study close to ten novels, alongside their theoretical background and historical context. This was no simple task, requiring countless hours of reading, memorizing, and synthesizing complex ideas.

The approach in the humanities is often linear. Students accumulate information in a step-by-step manner, building a comprehensive understanding of the subject matter. This accumulated knowledge then serves as the foundation for critical analysis and intellectual discourse.

Coding: A Different Kind of Challenge

In contrast, when studying for a programming course, I quickly realized that the approach was entirely different. There was minimal theory to memorize; instead, I was immediately thrown into solving coding problems. The learning process shifted from rote memorization to trial and error and the exercise of logical reasoning.

Unlike the humanities, where the goal is to acquire and apply knowledge, coding requires logic, reasoning, and a great deal of patience. The process is non-linear, often involving solving one task only to be faced with another, often unrelated task, which is part of a larger, more complex problem. For example, in a coding project, one might first need to clean a dataset, followed by textual analysis, and then move on to visualizing the data. Each of these tasks requires a different skill set and an accumulation of experience, rather than information.

Conclusion: Two Worlds, Two Sets of Challenges

While both coding and the humanities demand dedication and intellectual effort, the nature of these demands is quite different. The humanities rely on the accumulation of knowledge and critical thinking that lead to a solution, whereas coding relies on logic and perseverance to overcome a series of challenges. Understanding these differences can help bridge the gap between these two worlds and foster a deeper appreciation for the unique challenges each field presents.
